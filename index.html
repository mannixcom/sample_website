WIllaim Shakespear's Sonnets consists of 154 poems, each 14 lines long(three href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Couplet"couplet). Sonnet 18 is probably the most famous, and begins like this:

Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate
ROugh winds do shake the darling buds of May
And summer's lease hath all too short a date.
